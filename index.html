📖 Learn About Islam

"And whoever seeks a religion other than Islam, it will never be accepted of him, and in the Hereafter he will be one of the losers." [Quran 3:85]

Tawheed - The Oneness of Allah

The foundation of Islam is Tawheed - believing in the absolute Oneness of Allah in three categories:

1. Tawheed ar-Rububiyyah (Lordship)

Allah alone is the Creator, Sustainer, and Controller of all existence.

"Indeed, your Lord is Allah, who created the heavens and earth in six days and then established Himself above the Throne, arranging the matter..." [Quran 7:54]

2. Tawheed al-Uluhiyyah (Worship)

Allah alone deserves all worship and no act of worship should be directed to anyone else.

"And I did not create the jinn and mankind except to worship Me." [Quran 51:56]

3. Tawheed al-Asma wa as-Sifat (Names and Attributes)

Allah's Names and Attributes are unique to Him and perfect, without resemblance to creation.

"There is nothing like unto Him, and He is the All-Hearer, the All-Seer." [Quran 42:11]

The Five Pillars of Islam

🕌

1. Shahada (Declaration of Faith)

Arabic: أشهد أن لا إله إلا الله وأشهد أن محمداً رسول الله

English: "I bear witness that there is no god worthy of worship except Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah."

🤲

2. Salah (Prayer)

Five daily prayers: Fajr (dawn), Dhuhr (noon), Asr (afternoon), Maghrib (sunset), and Isha (night).

💰

3. Zakat (Obligatory Charity)

2.5% of savings given annually to purify wealth and help the needy (for those who meet the nisab threshold).

🌙

4. Sawm (Fasting in Ramadan)

Abstaining from food, drink, and marital relations from dawn to sunset during the month of Ramadan.

🕋

5. Hajj (Pilgrimage)

Pilgrimage to Mecca once in a lifetime for those who are physically and financially able.

The Six Articles of Faith

"Faith (Iman) is to believe in Allah, His Angels, His Books, His Messengers, the Last Day, and divine decree (Qadar), both good and bad." [Sahih Muslim 8]

  • 1. Belief in Allah: The One, Unique Creator without partners, sons, or daughters
  • 2. Belief in Angels: Noble beings created from light who worship Allah and carry out His commands
  • 3. Belief in the Books: Torah (Tawrat), Psalms (Zabur), Gospel (Injeel), and the Quran (final and preserved)
  • 4. Belief in the Messengers: From Adam to Muhammad (peace be upon them), with Muhammad ﷺ being the final messenger
  • 5. Belief in the Last Day: The Day of Resurrection when all will be judged by Allah
  • 6. Belief in Divine Decree: Allah's complete knowledge and control over all things, good and bad

Primary Sources of Islamic Knowledge

📕 The Noble Quran

The final revelation from Allah, preserved in its original Arabic form. Memorized by millions and unchanged for over 1400 years.

"Indeed, it is We who sent down the Quran and indeed, We will be its guardian." [Quran 15:9]

📚 Authentic Sunnah

The verified sayings, actions, and approvals of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, primarily found in Sahih Bukhari and Sahih Muslim.

👥 Ijma' of the Salaf

The consensus of the righteous predecessors (Salaf as-Salih) on matters of religion.
